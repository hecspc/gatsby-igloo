## What is Accessibility?[](#what-is-accessibility)

- - -

‌

Digital accessibility refers to the practice of building digital content and applications that can be used by a wide range of people, including individuals who have visual, hearing, motor or cognitive disabilities.

## Taylor Digital's Philosophy[](#taylor-digitals-philosophy)

- - -

‌

At Taylor Digital, we strive to meet the needs of as many individuals as possible. Making our products accessible is a benefit to everyone, regardless of abilities or impairments.

‌

Taylor Digital's Policy[](#taylor-digitals-policy)

- - -

‌

Taylor Digital is committed to ensuring that its website is accessible to people with disabilities. All the pages on our website will meet W3C WAI’s [Web Content Accessibility Guidelines](https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG20/) 2.0, Level AA conformance. Report any issues to [cncaya@taylordigital.io](mailto:cncaya@taylordigital.io).

WCAG 2.1 Principles[](#wcag-2-1-principles)

- - -

‌

WCAG 2.1 AA is an addition to WCAG 2.0. It adds 17 new requirements addressing the accessibility gaps of WCAG 2.0. At this stage, it is only a recommendation and is not required by law but probably will be in the future.

‌

Common Examples of Accessibility[](#common-examples-of-accessibility)

- - -

‌

Use of Text[](#use-of-text)

- - -

‌

All text should be legible and meet accessibility standards. The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG 2.0) level AA requires a 4.5.1 color contrast between text and background for normal text, and 3:1 to large text. Learn more about color, contrast, and accessibility design at Material Design Accessibility.

![](https://gblobscdn.gitbook.com/assets%2F-Lc7A9CHv6__8gx3y1JN%2F-Lem3XVqy-iRqDOZofp9%2F-Lem7il8k2gv4a34kRSo%2Fimage.png?alt=media&token=5a005caf-1b2c-4304-9b63-38fd4a804e98)

Use of Color[](#use-of-color)
